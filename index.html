Abdul Qadeer Khan

1936 - 2021

Missile Man of Pakistan

Abdul Qadeer Khan (April 27, 1936 â€“ October 10, 2021) was a Pakistani nuclear scientist recognized for his notable contributions to Pakistan's nuclear program. Born in Bhopal, British India (now in Madhya Pradesh, India), Khan moved to Pakistan after the 1947 partition. He studied metallurgical engineering in West Germany and later specialized in nuclear engineering in the Netherlands. Playing a pivotal role in Pakistan's nuclear weapons program, Abdul Qadeer Khan was instrumental in the successful nuclear tests in 1998, marking Pakistan's entry into the nuclear weapons community. Often referred to as the "Father of the Pakistani Nuclear Program," he significantly advanced the country's nuclear capabilities. Abdul Qadeer Khan's career involved various scientific and administrative roles, including his tenure at the Pakistan Atomic Energy Commission (PAEC). His expertise and leadership were crucial in shaping Pakistan's nuclear strategy and capabilities.
